History

In history of Madrid we can find a lot ot different information pages, but all that historys are not true.
Agerit (Madrid), "land rich in water", is the name with which the Arabs identified the messetarian enclave near the Sierra de Guadarrama that Felipe II chose to establish his court and that would later become the Madrid we know today.
The first historical evidence of the city dates from the year 865, when the emir Muhammed I ordered the construction of a citadel in the village of Mayrit, on the banks of the Manzanares River. Mayrit means in Arabic "abundance of rivers of water"

Elena

There is a well-known legend among the residents of Madrid, which tells a medieval mystery from the court of King Philip II, about a beautiful young woman named Elena.
Elena was married to a captain of the royal troops named Zapata, who was very loyal to the monarch. He was sent to fight in Flanders, where he died.
